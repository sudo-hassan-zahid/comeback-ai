# Access and Wellbeing Guide

## Internet access
Ask the institution about library computers, device-loan schemes, downloadable course packs, and offline submission arrangements. Download material while connected and tell teaching staff early if connectivity can affect a deadline.

## Long commute or work
Build a minimum viable weekly plan around fixed travel and work hours. Use commute time only for light tasks such as audio notes or flashcards. Ask an adviser whether timetable changes, recorded sessions, or flexible office hours are available.

## Stress and overwhelm
Reduce the immediate load to one small academic action and one conversation with a trusted person. Contact the institution's counselling or wellbeing service when stress persists or interferes with daily life. If someone may be in immediate danger, contact local emergency services or a trusted adult now.

## Asking for help
Use a specific request: explain the situation briefly, name the course or task, describe what has already been tried, and ask for one concrete form of support. Teachers and advisers can respond faster when the request includes a proposed meeting time or next step.
