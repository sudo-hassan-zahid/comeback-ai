# Study Support Playbook

## Low attendance
Speak privately with the student and ask about transport, health, care duties, safety, or timetable barriers. Agree on one realistic attendance goal for the next two weeks and identify a named staff contact. Avoid punishment-first responses; solve the barrier and review progress together.

## Missing assignments
Break overdue work into a short priority list: essential task, next action, and achievable deadline. Offer a check-in, a quiet study location, and examples of completed work. Confirm that the student understands submission tools before assuming low motivation.

## Study routine
Start with three focused 25-minute sessions each week. Put each session on a calendar, choose one concrete task, silence notifications, and finish by writing the next action. Increase the schedule only after it becomes consistent.

## Exam preparation
Use retrieval practice: close the notes and answer questions from memory, then check mistakes. Mix topics across sessions and space practice over several days. Past-paper questions and self-made flashcards are more useful than repeatedly rereading notes.
